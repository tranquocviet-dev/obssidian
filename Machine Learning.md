Machine learning is a simple concept stretched out by the various complicated names and definitions given to its subsections. In truth, the essence of it is just inputting data into a machine and wait for it to analyze it, and finally make judgements and adjustments based on such analitics. The process follow the usual `Input - Processing - Output - Evaluation` flow.

Machine learning is segmented into smaller parts done by a unit named [[Tensor]]. It is the machine's job to get info from the inputted data and provide insight on them.

Machine learning can be started with processed data[^1], unprocessed data or a combination of both. The goal is either to catch patterns or to predict and adjust accordingly, and so, it is not so much about the machine that makes the difference, rather more about the human deciding what to do with the statistics that machine learning gives them.

[^1]: [[Supervised Learning]]
