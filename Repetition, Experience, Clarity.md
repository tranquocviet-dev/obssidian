In [[Shadow Slave]], Neph mentioned the concept of repetition, experience and clarity. At first glance her words seem shallow and more or less just a saying for saying sake, but the more one thinks and ponders about it, the more they will realize the magnitude of her words and how they relate to the world we live in.

Repetition is a concept of practice, of learning. It is equivalent to the small homework we do in our off times, practicing and gaining an understanding of the problems we are given, and how to solve them when time comes. As important as that is, it is only one third of the cornerstones of the theory.

Experience in concept is the trials that we make use of our knowledge in. Think of them as the tests we take after learning, making use of the repetition before. An important, vital step, no doubt, but even when combined with repetition is still only a bit more than half the problem.

Clarity is the final thing we must take into consideration, the purpose. No lesson, no tests, exams, hardships that one has to experience can mean anything without the adequate clarity of why they have to do such things. Be it their goals, satisfaction, money, or anything else - one must find a purpose, or else their actions will instantly become aimless, unstable, and simply never yield results.

The foundation of knowledge is based on these 3 aspects. Without even one of them, the knowledge of one will become flawed and unsatisfactory.
