School, after all, is just a place for students to try and satisfy the demands of some other people who think they have the authority to judge what is right, what is wrong, what is acceptable and what is not. The cycle goes something like this: You are given a task, you do that task until (hopefully) you land on the jackpot slot when what you do is deemed "correct" by those people, and when you fail to do so, you get dismissed.

When people wonder about the "hivemind", it simply means people succumbing to the thought of what is true from other individuals. Eventually no one will have any sense of authenticity. What little creativeness one has will eventually be extinguished by the masses, turning to conform to the "standards" and kills thoughts.

School isnt a place to produce adults. [[The real purpose of school, from the start, is a place to produce mindless, obedient workers]][^1]. But at the same time, it is possible for you to resist it, despite not directly fighting back. Simply acknowledging this is a way for you to resist the urge to conform, confide, contain.
