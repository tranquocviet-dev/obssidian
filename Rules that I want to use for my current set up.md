In [[Steph Ango]]'s [[Style is consistent constraint]], he argues that having a consistent style accelerates work, and that having constraints to your work can actually increase your productivity and creativity, for you having to accomodate the constrainst. As such, I have come to borrow some of his guidelines, along with adding some of my own:
- I can change anything at any time, but have no obligation to
- Similar to Steph, I use `YYYY-MM-DD` everywhere and pluralize tag and folder names at all times
- I aim to write coherent thoughts inside of one file and expanding upon them with different ideas - one file, one idea, fully realized
- I avoid using wording of others inside my vault
According to the first rule, I can come back and edit these restrains later, remove or add more to suit my style of writing. I have no obligation to write, for [[My note is an extension of my thought]], not the other way around.
